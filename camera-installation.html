Camera Technologies

The Right Camera for Every Location

We work with industry-leading brands including Hikvision, Axis, Hanwha, and Dahua — selecting the ideal camera type for each coverage zone based on lighting conditions, distance, indoor/outdoor requirements, and budget.

Fixed Cameras

Ideal for monitoring a specific area with consistent, high-quality coverage. Perfect for doorways, hallways, registers, and fixed asset areas.

Varifocal Cameras

Adjustable focal length for flexible coverage — ideal when you need to zoom in on a specific zone without sacrificing field of view. Great for parking lots and open areas.

PTZ Cameras

Pan-Tilt-Zoom cameras provide dynamic, operator-controlled coverage across large areas. Used for warehouses, perimeters, and environments requiring active monitoring.

Dome Cameras

Vandal-resistant dome design for indoor and outdoor use. The 360° housing makes it difficult to determine where the camera is pointing — providing a strong visual deterrent.

IR / Low-Light Cameras

Infrared and low-light cameras maintain clear footage in complete darkness or challenging lighting environments — essential for exterior, parking, and 24/7 coverage areas.

Fisheye / 360° Cameras

Single-camera 360-degree coverage for open spaces like lobbies, showrooms, and warehouses. Reduces camera count while maximizing coverage — with dewarping for detailed review.

Cloud-Managed Cameras

Cloud-connected cameras for businesses that want simplified management, remote configuration, and off-site storage — no NVR required. Ideal for distributed or multi-site deployments.

PoE Cameras

Power over Ethernet cameras receive both power and data through a single cable — simplifying installation, reducing costs, and enabling centralized power management via PoE switches.

Professional structured cabling, camera mounting, PoE switching, and network integration — all neatly labeled, documented, and installed to commercial standards.
